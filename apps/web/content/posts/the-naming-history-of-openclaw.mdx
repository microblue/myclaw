# From Clawdbot to Moltbot to OpenClaw: The Full Naming History

OpenClaw is now one of the most popular open-source projects on GitHub with over 145,000 stars, but the name you know today is actually the third one. The project went through two rebrandings in the span of just three days — a wild ride that nearly tore the community apart but ultimately made it stronger.

Here's the full story.

## Chapter 1: Clawdbot (November 2025)

It all started in November 2025 when Austrian software engineer Peter Steinberger released an open-source AI assistant called **Clawdbot**. The name was a playful nod to Anthropic's Claude — "Clawd" with a lobster-themed twist, plus "bot" to signal it was an AI assistant.

Steinberger had previously built a personal AI assistant called **Clawd** (later renamed to **Molty**), and Clawdbot was the natural evolution — an open-source, self-hostable version that anyone could run on their own infrastructure.

The project took off immediately. Developers loved that they could host their own AI assistant without depending on a third-party service. The lobster branding was quirky and memorable. Stars piled up on GitHub. Everything seemed great.

Until Anthropic's legal team got involved.

## Chapter 2: Moltbot (January 27, 2026)

In January 2026, Anthropic filed trademark complaints against the project. The name "Clawdbot" was considered too close to "Claude" — and legally speaking, they had a point. The similar phonetics could cause brand confusion.

Steinberger didn't fight it. On **January 27, 2026**, he announced the project would be renamed to **Moltbot**. The new name kept the lobster heritage — "molting" is the process of lobsters shedding their shells to grow — while distancing itself from any Claude association.

The community reaction was mixed. Some appreciated the clever biological reference. Others thought it sounded awkward. "Moltbot" didn't exactly roll off the tongue, and the memes were... unkind.

But the code was solid, the community was growing, and a name is just a name. Right?

## Chapter 3: OpenClaw (January 30, 2026)

Three days. That's how long "Moltbot" lasted.

On **January 30, 2026**, Steinberger announced the final rebrand to **OpenClaw**. The reasoning was straightforward:

- **"Open"** — reflecting the project's open-source, community-driven nature
- **"Claw"** — preserving the lobster heritage that the community had embraced from day one

The name stuck. It was clean, memorable, and professional. It honored the project's roots without stepping on any trademarks. The community rallied behind it, and OpenClaw quickly became the definitive name.

## Why It Matters

The naming saga of OpenClaw is more than trivia. It highlights a real tension in the open-source world: how do you build a community identity when the legal landscape can pull the rug out from under you?

Steinberger handled it with grace. Rather than fighting expensive legal battles, he listened to the community, iterated quickly, and landed on a name that's arguably better than the original. The three-day turnaround from Moltbot to OpenClaw showed a project leader who wasn't precious about branding — he cared about the code and the community more than the name on the tin.

Today, OpenClaw has an active ecosystem of tools and integrations, and a community that spans the globe. Some community projects still carry traces of the old names in their descriptions, a testament to the rapid evolution.
